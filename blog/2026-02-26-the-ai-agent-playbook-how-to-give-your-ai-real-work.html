The AI Agent Playbook: How to Give Your AI Real Work

February 26, 2026 · 8 min read

Back to blog Back to home

Most teams call AI and start chatting. Useful, but limited. Real leverage starts when your AI has a defined role, standing instructions, and permission to execute repeatable work with memory and context.

What makes AGENTS.md and SOUL.md effective

A strong agent configuration doesn’t read like marketing copy. It defines scope, constraints, task priorities, quality bars, and fallback behavior. The goal is not personality. The goal is reliable output under pressure.

  • AGENTS.md: operational instructions, tools, and execution boundaries.
  • SOUL.md: voice, style, values, and long-term behavioral defaults.

Chat vs agency

Chat is reactive. Agency is proactive. In chat mode, a model answers one prompt at a time. In agency mode, the system can plan, run tasks, verify outcomes, and continue until completion.

If you want business impact, design for agency: explicit outcomes, measurable checks, and handoff rules.

Memory, persistence, and standing orders

Good agents keep state. They remember unresolved tasks, standardize recurring flows, and accumulate operational memory over time. Persistence turns one-off wins into durable systems.

Standing orders are the missing piece. They encode what the agent should always do without being asked every time.
